The Breathtaking Scenery of the Alahmbra

The Stunning Architecture of Islamic Culture in Spain

Upon it's origins in 1238, the Alhambra was once it's own city, seperated from Granada. As a major royal city, it contained at least six palaces. The famously preserved palaces are the: Comares Palace, Palace of the Lions, and the Partal Place. In 1870 the Alhambra was declared a National Monument of Spain and the state allocated a budget for its conservation, overseen by the Provincial Commission of Monuments.

Discover the Beauty of The Granada Cathedral

The Grenada Cathedral, officially known as the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, is a world renound Catholic church located in St. George's, the capital city of Grenada. Arriving in the mid-19th century, the cathedral stands as a historical and architectural landmark, showcasing Gothic Revival design elements. Its striking appeareance is decorated with intricate details, making it a significant religious landmark in Grenada.

Stunning Beauty of The Nasrid Palaces Upon the Granada Hilltops

The Nasrid Palaces, built within the Alhambra complex in Granada, Spain, represent an exquisite example of Islamic art and architecture. Built during the the 13th to the 15th centuries, these palaces showcase intricate geometric designs, and vibrant tile mosaics. Visitors to the Nasrid Palaces can explore the Alhambra's rich history while marveling at the stunning craftsmanship that reflects the cultural and artistic achievements of Islamic culture in Spain.

Granada Is A Must For Your Next Travel Destination

Granada's charming streets, vibrant neighborhoods, and a fusion of Moorish and Spanish influences make it a captivating destination for tourists and history enthusiasts alike. Granada is renowned for its rich history, stunning architecture, and cultural significance.
